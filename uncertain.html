WORK WITH UNCERTAINITY

Underestimating uncertainty can lead to strategies that neither defend against the threats nor take advantage of the opportunities that higher levels of uncertainty may provide.

Assuming that everything in the world is completely unpredictable can cause managers to completely give up on the analytical rigour of their traditional planning processes and base their strategic choices almost exclusively on intuition.

Risk-averse managers experience decision paralysis because they don't trust their gut feelings and believe they are operating in very uncertain conditions. They avoid making important strategic choices regarding the goods, markets, and technology they ought to create.


FEATURES OF THIS COURSE


  • A Clear-Enough Future. This is 1st level in which strategic judgments are unaffected by residual uncertainty.

  • Alternate Futures. Level 2 describes the future as one of several potential outcomes, or discrete scenarios.

  • Range of futures A variety of potential futures can be found at level 3. The actual result could be located anywhere along a continuum that is bounded by that range, which is specified by a small number of crucial variables.

  • True Ambiguity. At level 4, a number of interacting variables of uncertainty produce a situation that is nearly impossible to foresee.
