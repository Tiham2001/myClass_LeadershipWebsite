SUCCESSION PLANNING

The process of selecting personnel who can take over your company's important positions is known as succession planning. It aids in locating potential capacity and competence shortages as well as addressing the need for professional development. It allows for career conversations with workers.

Planning for succession assures the long-term viability of your business. Good employees leave every organisation for a variety of personal and professional reasons. To ensuring that your company is in a position to carry out its goals and strategy with a high-performing staff, succession planning is crucial.

Such planning ought to be in line with the overall business strategy; doing so will guarantee that the organization's investment in potential leaders is focused and reflects its strategic direction. Most significantly, succession planning requires a continuous commitment to ensure that the greatest talent is placed in the appropriate positions


FEATURES OF THIS COURSE


  • Identify Key Positions Analyse business plans to identify the positions most critical for business growth

  • Determine Future Successors Determine the essential skills needed for these professions and the high-potential candidates who could take over these responsibilities.

  • Identify the readiness date for any potential successors. Be aware of when your replacement will be prepared to take over. Are they prepared to start development right away?

  • Analyze their developmental requirements. Identify the training and development needed to close skill gaps and get them ready for promotion. This can include external courses, secondments, on-the-job training, and mentorship.

  • Make A Permanent Commitment All organisational managers should always look for talent gaps and concentrate on the advancement of top performers.
