ACCOUNTABILITY

Accountability is the quality of being held accountable for a system's actions, behaviours, and prospective effects. Accountability is the acceptance of responsibility for deeds, choices, and outcomes.

Accountability is the willingness or readiness to explain or justify one's decisions, intentions, and actions to stakeholders.

Together with your team, establish an accountability culture. When you can rely on your staff to put their best work forward, you have an accountable culture. Learn why it's important to encourage accountability and ownership at work.


FEATURES OF THIS COURSE


  • Treating it as your responsibility to be accountable A style of thinking about how to accept responsibility for your life and ownership of your decisions, accountability is an attitude.

  • Personal accountability development If the endorsement of your manager isn't quite enough to spur you on to make the extra effort you need to advance, perhaps you're prepared to work on your own personal accountability.

  • Developing as a Professional Accountable Everyone who works for a company reports to someone else, unless they are the only proprietor. Every employee has a superior, whether it be the CEO or the accounting temp. The boss's opinion and approval, however, are insufficient on their own to make a job satisfying. Everyone also has an inner boss, a private, internal voice that directs you as to whether the work is done as effectively as you are capable of doing it.
